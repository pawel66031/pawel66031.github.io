Hexa Mirror

Hexa Mirror

Role in the project: Gameplay programmer & level design tool developer

Hexa Mirror is a puzzle game developed for Hello GameJam 2023. It tells the story of a young boy invited to a nearby circus, only to encounter an evil clown who turns people into briefcases. The player's goal is to navigate the hexagonal grid, avoid deadly lasers, and ultimately defeat the clown to put an end to his attacks.

The core mechanic revolves around movement on a hexagonal board. However, due to time constraints, the game lacked a proper pathfinding algorithm, which made controlling the character less intuitive. This aspect frustrated some players and contributed to the game's lower challenge level, as the logic-based puzzles did not reach their full potential.

I was responsible for all gameplay programming, as well as developing a custom level design tool used by the artists to streamline the creation of levels (placing lasers, mirrors, and obstacles). One of the biggest challenges was time pressure and technical difficulties—my old Linux-based machine struggled with low FPS in Unity due to driver issues, making development harder. In the final stretch, I coordinated with one of the artists to compile and finalize the project, ensuring we could fix last-minute bugs before the deadline.

Despite its shortcomings, Hexa Mirror placed 9th out of 14 entries in the jam. While the controls and challenge level received mixed feedback, the game was praised for its visual style. The project was a valuable learning experience in game development workflows, rapid prototyping, and tool creation for level design.

The game is playable in itch.io: Hexa Mirror
